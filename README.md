# Business Intelligence Study Guide

## 1. Introduction to Business Intelligence

Business Intelligence (BI) is a broad term that encompasses architectures, tools, databases, methodologies and applications that allow interactive access to data in order to support strategic decision-making.

BI transforms raw data into meaningful business information.

Traditionally, BI focuses on descriptive analytics, helping organizations understand what happened and what is currently happening in the business environment.

---

## 2. Core Concepts

### ETL (Extract, Transform and Load)

Process responsible for:
- extracting data from multiple sources;
- transforming and cleaning inconsistent information;
- loading structured data into a centralized repository.

### Data Warehouse (DW)

A centralized repository optimized for analytical queries and historical business analysis.

### OLTP vs OLAP

| OLTP | OLAP |
|------|------|
| Operational systems | Analytical systems |
| Fast transactions | Complex queries |
| Daily operations | Strategic analysis |

### Dimensional Modeling

Technique used to organize analytical data using:
- Fact Tables;
- Dimension Tables.

Most common models:
- Star Schema;
- Snowflake Schema.

### Data Mining

Process of discovering patterns, trends and relationships in large datasets.
